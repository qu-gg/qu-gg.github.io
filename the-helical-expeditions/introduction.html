Introduction

Written & Edited by Quagg
Many adventures adapted from One-Shot Wonders by Roll-N-Play Press
Dear Fellow Adventurers,

Welcome to The Helical Expeditions! This is a set of ready-to-use one-shot adventures set within the TTRPG BREAK!!. I made this collection to show off the diversity of adventures and themes one can have within its lore, and to have a set for myself to run. Each adventure represents my attempt to connect deeper with the setting and have fun getting lost in imagining what's there and what stories come and go.

BREAK!! is the first system I've really felt comfortable breaking apart and crafting my own stories in (no pun intended). It's such an endearing world that you can slot anything into and have it mesh seamlessly. I owe a lot to the community for the support they've given around my creations (and tools!).

Whether you're just looking for fresh material or new to the Outer World entirely, I hope these expeditions spark joy and inspire memorable sessions at your table as they have mine.

Enjoy!

— Quagg

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions
Does every adventure include all aspects of BREAK!! rules (Journey, Exploration, Negotiation, Combat)?
No, and intentionally so. Each adventure's set of rules used is molded around the scope of the adventure. Many adventures can be completed without violence at all. Some focus heavily on exploration and discovery, others on social intrigue, and some on fighting wondrous foes in wondrous locations.
What are your inspirations?
While many of the story-beats themselves stem from One-Shot Wonders by Roll-N-Play Press, the re-flavor inspirations stem from my favorite media. To name a few: BREAK!!, Final Fantasy, Adventure Time, Gravity Falls, Avatar: The Last Airbender, Nier, Moebius, the OSR Community, and the list goes on and on. I hope the whimsy and imagination of these amazing works peek through in my adaptations.
Use of Generative AI?
No generative AI was used in writing or considered for art sources — just human brainwaves there. Standard coding agents were used to help with some of the more funky aspects of front-end formatting and development. All creative content, story adaptation, and artistic direction came from good old-fashioned imagination and countless hours of tinkering.
